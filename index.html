When We Were Gun

Fleur-de-Lis Press, 2025

Winner of the 2025 Louisville Review National Poetry Prize

Deborah Schupack's fourth book, When We Were Gun, occupies a narrative space between poetry and prose and a psychic space between everyday and end-of-the-world. The twenty narrative poems are told in a "Greek chorus" of parent voices as they wait outside the scene of a school shooting, forging a remarkable chronicle of the ordinary exigency of parenting.

Relentless

Mount Sinai Health System, 2022

Written while the world was still in the grips of the COVID-19 pandemic, Relentless tells the capacious and compelling story of one of America's leading health care systems mobilizing across science, medicine and the human experience to battle the greatest healthcare challenge in living memory. The book is based on more than 100 interviews with doctors, nurses, scientists, patients, hospital leaders, and many others deployed to the front lines of the pandemic response.

This nonfiction narrative reads like a novel as it documents the science, medicine and human experience on the front lines of the greatest healthcare challenge in living memory.

About

Writer, verbal strategist, and content creator at the intersection of narrative craft and strategic communication.

As an author

Deborah has published award-winning books of fiction, poetry and narrative nonfiction. Her latest, When We Were Gun (Fleur-de-Lis Press, 2025), won the Louisville Review National Poetry Contest and has been nominated for a Pushcart Prize. Her first novel, The Boy on the Bus (Free Press/Simon & Schuster, 2003), received wide-spread critical acclaim, including, from the author James Patterson: "my favorite book this year." Her second novel, Sylvan Street (Penguin, 2010), was optioned for television by ABC studios. In addition, Deborah spent the pandemic reporting and writing the narrative science book Relentless (Mount Sinai, 2022), chronicling New York City's medical and scientific response to Covid. The book debuted as Amazon's #1 book in Healthcare Administration and is available commercially as well as from medical school libraries across the country.

As a verbal strategist and copywriter

Deborah creates compelling messaging and thought-leadership content at the intersection of science, medicine, and the human experience. Her background as a journalist and novelist infuses voice-driven content and messaging, connecting the rapidly evolving world of healthcare and its outsize influence on our everyday lives. She writes, edits and consults for major healthcare organizations, foundations, and branding agencies— positioning organizations in a fast-moving industry, guiding new products and initiatives to market, and communicating healthcare innovations to various stakeholders and audiences.
